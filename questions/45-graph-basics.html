Discrete Mathematics / Graph Theory

In an undirected graph, the sum of degrees of all vertices is equal to:

Discrete Mathematics Graph Theory Graph Basics
MCQ2026Practice

Choose an Option

Answer

Twice the number of edges

Theory

Handshaking Lemma

The sum of degrees of all vertices in an undirected graph equals twice the number of edges.

Solution

  • Each edge contributes degree 1 to two vertices.
  • Hence every edge contributes 2 to total degree.
  • Total degree sum = 2E.
  • Therefore the answer is twice the number of edges.
